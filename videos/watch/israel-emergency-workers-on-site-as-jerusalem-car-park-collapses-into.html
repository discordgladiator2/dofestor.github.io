Emergency workers could be seen operating within the cordoned-off area of the Shaare Zedek Medical Centre in Jerusalem on Monday, after parts of the hospital car park collapsed into a giant sinkhole earlier in the day.

The footage also shows the asphalt and earth continuing to crumble, causing alarm that the sinkhole could continue to expand further.

No one was injured after the sinkhole opened up after tunnels were being dug for a nearby motorway.

Israeli police declared the area around the hospital as a danger zone. No injuries or casualties have been reported so far. An investigation into the causes of the collapse continues.
