Thomas E. Clancy, MD, Associate Chief of Surgical Oncology at Brigham and Women’s Hospital (BWH), performs a robotic hepatectomy, one of the first in Boston.

BWH is a world leader in using state-of-the-art robotics to significantly shorten patient recovery time and to minimize or eliminate the physical challenges presented by traditional laparoscopic (minimally invasive) surgery. As one of the most experienced and diversified robotic surgery providers worldwide, the largest multispecialty robotic surgery program in Boston, and the first center in the Boston region to perform 2,000 robot-assisted surgeries, we are doing everything possible to provide patients with the most appropriate surgical option.
